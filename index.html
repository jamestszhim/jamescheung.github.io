IVEC 2016

Financial Technology

IVEC is a competition organized by three top universities across the globe. Students in these universities work together and build a start-up plan under the area, financial inclusion. The competition works as a wonderful platform for us working with teammates having very different culture and background.

In June, we traveled to Singapore and met our great teams there, we have 5 group members coming from 3 different universities with 4 nationality, each studying a different major. We together attended numerous workshops and lecture about the topic and consulted various industry expert and venture capitalist about our business plan. We are pround to bring the idea on desk and eventually won the 2nd place.

The idea we suggested is a service providing short term loans for those with little to no credit histories, letting the underbanked leverage their social network as guarantors to borrow money at a lower interest rate than traditional payday lenders. Both borrowers and guarantors build credit at the same time.

Work hard, play hard. Apart from the competition, we also looked around the city. In general Singapore is a great place to explore, with lots of good food and energetic people.

  • Date: June 2016
  • Organization: HKUST (Hong Kong), NTU (Singapore), UCB (United States)
  • Area: Marketing, Finance, Start-up, Tech, Blockchain

IVEC 2015

Water for Life

Same as 2016, IVEC is a great competition for collaboration among diverse students. In 2015, our topic was "Water". Water resources are limited and precious, under climate change and global warming, the problem of water scarcity is worsen especially in less developed countries. The competition aimed to relief the problem by bringing up innovative idea about water technology.

We went to Singapore, which is a country with advanced water technology. Our team come up with an idea to raise fund for water startups, by quantifying ones' donations and effort in solving the problem using the term, "social share". It was like gamifying the funding process and creating incentive for people to donate. The judges liked our idea and eventually we got the 2nd place.

  • Date: June 2016
  • Organization: HKUST (Hong Kong), NTU (Singapore), UCB (United States)
  • Area: Marketing, Finance, Start-up, Tech, Water Technology, Design Thinking
